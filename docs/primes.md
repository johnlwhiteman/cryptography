# Prime Numbers

## Definitions

A natural number is a positive integer greater than 0 such that:

$$\N = \{1,2,3,...,\infty\}$$

A whole number is number 0 or greater such that:

$$\N = \{0,1,2,3,...,\infty\}$$

Hint: A whole number has has "hole" or "oh" or "O".

A prime number is a positive integer greater than 1 that is evenly divided by itself and 1 only.

A composite number is a positive integer greater than 1 that is not prime.

The numbers 0 and 1 are neither prime or composite.

There are an infinite number of prime numbers.

2 is the only even prime number.

2 and 3 are the only consecutive numbers which are prime.

Every even number greater than 2 can be express as the sum of two primes (Goldbach Conjecture).

When multiplying two prime numbers other than 2, the result will always be an odd number.
